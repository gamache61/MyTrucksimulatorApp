Air Brake Tests & Procedures (What to know)

A) Air Pressure Build Test

Start engine, build air to governor cut‑out. Watch dual gauges rise (primary/secondary). Many courses teach build rate and cut‑out range (commonly around 120–135 PSI).

  • At cut‑out you may hear the air dryer purge.
  • If pressure won’t build: check compressor, governor, leaks, or frozen moisture.
B) Applied Leakage Test (60 seconds)

With engine off, key on if required, service brake applied and held for 60 seconds. Pressure drop must be within your state/province limits.

  • Listen for leaks at valves, lines, chambers, fittings, glad hands.
  • Know that leak limits differ (single vehicle vs combination vehicle).

Your simulator uses a 60-second timer to practice the sequence and attention to gauge movement.

C) Low Air Warning Test

Fan off air pressure (brake pedal) until warning activates (commonly around ~60 PSI).

  • Warning can be light, buzzer, or both.
  • Must activate before pressure gets too low.
D) Spring Brake Pop‑Out / Emergency Brake Test

Continue fanning until the spring brakes apply / knobs pop out (commonly ~20–45 PSI).

  • Know what the yellow (park) and red (trailer supply) controls do.
  • Never drive with spring brakes applied; they can overheat and be damaged.
