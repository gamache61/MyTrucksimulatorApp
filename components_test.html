Air Brake Tests & Procedures

A) Air Pressure Build Test

Start engine, build air to governor cut‑out. Watch dual gauges rise (primary/secondary). Cut-out commonly around 120–135 PSI.

  • At cut‑out you may hear the air dryer purge.
  • If pressure won't build: check compressor, governor, leaks, or frozen moisture.
B) Applied Leakage Test (60 seconds)

Engine off, service brake applied and held for 60 seconds. Pressure drop must be within limits.

  • Listen for leaks at valves, lines, chambers, fittings, glad hands.
  • Leak limits differ (single vs combination vehicle).
C) Low Air Warning Test

Fan off air until warning activates (commonly around ~60 PSI).

  • Warning can be light, buzzer, or both.
  • Must activate before pressure gets too low.
D) Spring Brake Pop‑Out / Emergency Brake Test

Continue fanning until spring brakes apply / knobs pop out (commonly ~20–45 PSI).

  • Know what yellow (park) and red (trailer supply) controls do.
  • Never drive with spring brakes applied.
